Three Kingdoms Story

The Peach Garden Oath

The Story of Three Brothers

In the peach(桃子) /piːtʃ/ garden(花园) /ˈɡɑːrdən/, three brothers(兄弟) /ˈbrʌðərz/ met for the first time. Liu Bei, Guan Yu, and Zhang Fei became friends(朋友) /frendz/ and made a promise(承诺) /ˈprɑːmɪs/. They swore(发誓) /swɔːr/ to work(工作) /wɜːrk/ together(一起) /təˈɡeðər/ to save(拯救) /seɪv/ the world(世界) /wɜːrld/. This decision(决定) /dɪˈsɪʒən/ changed(改变) /tʃeɪndʒd/ their lives(生活) /laɪvz/ and history(历史) /ˈhɪstəri/ forever.

Logic Question 1: Why and What Happens

Why did the three brothers become friends?

A. Because they wanted to save the world together
B. Because they liked the same food
C. Because they were from the same city
D. Because they had the same name

Logic Question 2: Story Sequence

What happened first in the story?

A. They met in the peach garden
B. They made a promise
C. They saved the world
D. They changed history

Journey to the West Story

Monkey King's Adventure

The Story of Monkey King

The monkey(猴子) /ˈmʌŋki/ king was very clever(聪明的) /ˈklevər/ and strong(强壮的) /strɔːŋ/. He could fly(飞) /flaɪ/ through the clouds(云) /klaʊdz/ and fight(战斗) /faɪt/ with magic(魔法) /ˈmædʒɪk/. One day, he met a monk(和尚) /mʌŋk/ who needed help(帮助) /help/. The monkey king decided to protect(保护) /prəˈtekt/ the monk on a long journey(旅程) /ˈdʒɜːrni/.

Logic Question 1: Character Traits

What made the monkey king special?

A. He was clever and could use magic
B. He was very tall and big
C. He had many friends
D. He liked to eat bananas

Logic Question 2: Story Purpose

Why did the monkey king help the monk?

A. Because the monk needed protection on a journey
B. Because the monk gave him food
C. Because the monk was his friend
D. Because the monk was very strong

Chinese Idiom Story

The Tortoise and the Hare

The Story of Slow and Steady

A hare(兔子) /her/ and a tortoise(乌龟) /ˈtɔːrtəs/ decided to have a race(比赛) /reɪs/. The hare was very fast(快的) /fæst/ and confident(自信的) /ˈkɑːnfɪdənt/. The tortoise was slow(慢的) /sloʊ/ but steady(稳定的) /ˈstedi/. During the race, the hare took a nap(小睡) /næp/ because he thought he would win(赢) /wɪn/ easily. But the tortoise kept moving(移动) /ˈmuːvɪŋ/ and won the race!

Logic Question 1: Character Comparison

What was the difference between the hare and the tortoise?

A. The hare was fast but lazy, the tortoise was slow but steady
B. The hare was small, the tortoise was big
C. The hare was brown, the tortoise was green
D. The hare was young, the tortoise was old

Logic Question 2: Story Lesson

What lesson does this story teach us?

A. Slow and steady wins the race
B. Fast animals are always better
C. Sleeping is important during races
D. Tortoises are smarter than hares
